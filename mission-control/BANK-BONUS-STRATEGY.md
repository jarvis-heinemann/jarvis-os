## 💰 Strategy: Churning Bank Accounts

### Method 1: Direct Deposit Method
**Best for:** Regular income/payroll

1. Find offers requiring direct deposit ($1K-$5K)
2. Set up payroll DD to new account
3. Keep open for required time (60-90 days)
4. Close or convert to no-fee account
5. Move to next offer

**Pros:** Easy if you have payroll
**Cons:** Takes 2-3 months per offer

### Method 2: Large Deposit Method
**Best for:** Have cash on hand

1. Find offers requiring $10K-$50K deposit
2. Move existing funds to new account
3. Hold for required time (30-90 days)
4. Collect bonus
5. Move to next offer

**Pros:** Fast, no DD needed
**Cons:** Requires capital

### Method 3: Real Direct Deposit Workaround
**Workarounds for DD requirement:**
- **Payroll split:** Split DD between banks
- **ACH transfers:** Some banks count ACH as DD (test first)
- **Venmo/PayPal:** Transfer to bank (sometimes counts)
- **Gig income:** Uber, DoorDash, etc. payouts

**Banks known to accept ACH as DD:**
- Capital One
- Discover
- Some credit unions

## ⚠️ Important Rules

### Churning Rules (Varies by Bank)
- **Chase:** 1 bonus every 2 years per product
- **Wells Fargo:** 1 bonus every 12 months
- **Citibank:** 1 bonus every 24 months
- **Capital One:** No hard limit
- **Bank of America:** 1 bonus every 12 months

### Credit Impact
- **Hard inquiries:** 2-5 point drop each
- **New accounts:** Temporary score decrease
- **Churning:** Too many can flag you as "gamer"
- **Recommendation:** Max 4-6 new accounts per year

### Tax Implications
- Bank bonuses are **taxable income** (1099-INT)
- Banks report to IRS
- Set aside ~25% for taxes
