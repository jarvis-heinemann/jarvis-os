## Key Updates from New Research

### 1. Connecticut Licensing Requirements

**Important finding:** Connecticut does NOT require a general contractor license at the state level. However:

- **Business Registration:** Must register with CT Secretary of State
- **Home Improvement Contractor (HIC):** Required for work on existing residential properties exceeding $1,000
  - Registration fee: ~$220 annually
  - Requires liability insurance
- **Specialty Licenses:** May be needed for electrical, plumbing, HVAC
- **Municipality-level:** Some towns (especially Fairfield County) may have additional requirements

**Source:** ProHouzz, ProCore, ServiceTitan (2025)

### 2. The Process: Two Approaches

From Connecticut contractor High Caliber Contracting:

**Lift-in-Place:**
- Raise home straight up, build new foundation underneath, set back down
- Faster, simpler, lower cost
- Best for: code compliance, new basement, straightforward sites
- Shorter crane time, fewer inspections

**Lift-and Lift house-Shift:**
-, relocate to different spot on property
- More planning (route, utilities, new footing locations)
- Best for: low/wet yards, properties with ledge, poor original orientation
- Higher cost but solves problems lift-in-place can't

**Key Insight:** Most elevation jobs use lift-in-place. Lift-and-shift is niche.

### 3. FEMA Grant Programs (Critical for Customers)

**Hazard Mitigation Grant Program (HMGP)**
- Post-disaster funding (when CT has Presidential disaster declaration)
- "Structure elevation" is explicitly eligible
- Homeowner cannot apply directly - goes through state/local government
- Must meet cost-effectiveness and feasibility requirements

**Flood Mitigation Assistance (FMA)**
- Competitive grant program
- For NFIP-insured buildings
- Reducing repetitive flood loss
- Also requires state/local application

**Key Point for Your Business:**
- Homeowners may qualify for 25-75% cost coverage through FEMA grants
- This makes the $250k job more affordable for them
- Understanding grants = competitive advantage when selling jobs

### 4. Insurance Requirements

**General Liability:**
- Standard for contractors: $1M-$2M coverage
- For specialized structural lifting: higher premiums expected
- CT requires workers' compensation if you have employees

**Cost Estimates (national averages):**
- General liability (basic): $500-$1,500/year
- General liability (with structural/elevated work): $2,000-$5,000+/year
- Workers' comp: varies by payroll
- Specialty insurance for house lifting: significantly higher

**Source:** The Hartford, Next Insurance, ContractorNerd

### 5. Connecticut Market Specifics

**FEMA Flood Maps:**
- Fairfield County flood maps under active revision
- Darien, CT has published updated preliminary maps (see darienct.gov/1553)
- Base Flood Elevation (BFE) requirements vary by zone
- CT ECO (cteco.uconn.edu) has all county map data

**Key Towns with High Flood Risk:**
- Fairfield, Westport, Norwalk, Darien, New Canaan (coastal)
- Properties in AE/VE zones require elevation to BFE

## Updated Business Model Assessment

### Option 1: GC/Project Manager (Recommended)
- **Startup Cost:** $20,000 - $50,000
- **What you need:**
  - CT business registration
  - HIC registration (~$220/year)
  - General liability insurance ($2,000-$5,000/year)
  - relationships with lifting contractors + foundation subs
- **Timeline to first job:** 2-4 months

### Option 2: Full-Service Contractor
- **Startup Cost:** $300,000 - $500,000+
- **What you need:**
  - Equipment (hydraulic lift systems: $100K-$300K)
  - Trucks/trailers: $50K-$150K
  - Full insurance package: $20K-$50K/year
  - Employees/crew
- **Timeline to first job:** 6-12 months

### Option 3: Consultant/Navigator
- **Startup Cost:** $5,000 - $10,000
- **What you do:**
  - Help homeowners navigate permits, engineers, contractors
  - Grant application assistance
  - Project management/oversight
- **Rate:** $50-$150/hour or flat fee per project
